1. Purpose of the Honor Code

The StudyGlade Honor Code protects learning. StudyGlade exists to help users understand, practice, revise, improve, and receive academic guidance. It does not exist to help users cheat, deceive instructors, submit another person’s work as their own, bypass academic rules, fabricate evidence, or complete restricted assessments dishonestly.

2. Good‑Faith Learning Standard

Good faith: Using StudyGlade to learn a concept, understand a method, review examples, receive feedback, practice academic skills, improve grammar, organize study materials, prepare for assessments through lawful study, or seek tutoring support allowed by the user’s institution.

Bad faith: Using StudyGlade to mislead, copy, cheat, hide authorship, bypass rules, complete restricted assessments, submit tutor work as personal work where prohibited, upload stolen materials, or use AI outputs to fabricate sources or false data.

3. Student Honor Duties

Students agree to use StudyGlade support responsibly. Students should check institutional rules before using tutor help, documents, AI tools, examples, templates, or study materials. Students may use support to understand and improve. Students may not use StudyGlade to dishonestly complete live exams, timed quizzes, proctored assessments, closed‑book work, or assignments that must be completed without outside assistance.

Students may not ask tutors to fabricate references, invent statistics, write false data, hide plagiarism, or produce work intended for dishonest submission. Students remain responsible for final academic choices and compliance with school or university rules.

4. Tutor Honor Duties

Tutors agree to teach, explain, guide, and support learning. Tutors may provide examples, explanations, draft feedback, problem‑solving guidance, research support, formatting support, and study assistance where allowed. Tutors may not help students cheat or complete restricted assessments.

Tutors must refuse requests for live exam help, proctored test answers, restricted quiz completion, fabricated sources, false references, false data, plagiarism, or off‑platform academic misconduct. Tutors must submit original work, verify sources where required, and use AI only as a reviewed support tool.

(i) Refusal of Prohibited Requests

A tutor who receives a prohibited request should refuse clearly and keep the response inside StudyGlade. The tutor should not complete part of the prohibited task, negotiate off‑platform, ask for private payment, or suggest ways to avoid detection. Where the request creates serious academic integrity or safety concerns, the tutor may report the matter to StudyGlade.

(ii) Honest Representation of Ability

Tutors should bid only on work they can reasonably complete within their approved subject knowledge, time availability, and skill level. A tutor should not accept work by claiming expertise they do not have, promising impossible deadlines, or relying on unverified AI output to cover lack of subject knowledge.

5. Document Seller Honor Duties

Document sellers agree to upload lawful study materials intended for learning. Sellers may not upload stolen assignments, copyrighted textbooks without permission, test banks, instructor‑only files, restricted exams, private course portal materials, private student information, or documents designed to help users cheat.

6. Buyer Honor Duties

Buyers may use unlocked documents for personal study, revision, understanding, practice, and learning. Buyers may not resell, redistribute, upload, publish, or submit unlocked documents as original work where this violates academic rules. Buying access to a document does not transfer ownership of the document to the buyer.

7. Artificial Intelligence Honor Duties

Users may use StudyGlade AI tools for learning support, grammar review, clarity improvement, similarity awareness, study explanation, and drafting help where allowed. Users may not use AI tools to cheat, fabricate sources, create false data, bypass academic integrity rules, or hide authorship in prohibited ways.

8. Honor Code Enforcement

StudyGlade may remove content, cancel questions, restrict tool access, deny refunds, hold payments, suspend tutor access, remove documents, restrict seller accounts, warn students, or close accounts where the Honor Code is violated. Serious or repeated violations may lead to permanent account closure.

9. Examples of Allowed Use

Asking a tutor to explain a concept

Checking how to solve a similar practice problem

Reviewing a draft for clarity where permitted

Learning how to cite sources

Unlocking notes for revision

Using a grammar tool to improve readability

Asking for a study plan before an assessment

Allowed use focuses on learning, understanding, practice, preparation, revision, and responsible academic development.

10. Examples of Prohibited Use

Asking a tutor to complete a live exam

Buying a document and submitting it as original work

Asking for fake references

Asking AI to invent data

Uploading a test bank

Selling another student’s assignment

Requesting direct payment outside StudyGlade

Asking a tutor to hide plagiarism

Prohibited use focuses on deception, cheating, false authorship, unfair academic advantage, privacy abuse, copyright infringement, or payment avoidance.

11. Honor in Communication

Users should communicate truthfully and respectfully. Students should not pressure tutors to break rules. Tutors should not pressure students for ratings or off‑platform payment. Sellers should not mislead buyers about document quality. Buyers should not file false complaints after receiving access. Honor includes honesty in messages, ratings, reports, payment claims, copyright notices, and refund requests.

12. Honor in Payments

Users honor the platform by keeping payments inside StudyGlade, paying for services before receiving paid access, avoiding chargeback abuse, refusing fake sessions, and not manipulating wallet records. Tutors and sellers honor the platform by earning only from genuine work and lawful document sales. Payment dishonesty may lead to stronger enforcement because it affects other users, platform trust, and financial records.

13. Honor in Documents

Users should upload and use documents responsibly. Sellers should share only lawful, useful, and accurately described study materials. Buyers should use unlocked materials for personal study unless a wider license is stated. A document that helps users understand a topic supports learning. A document uploaded or used to cheat, copy, mislead, or infringe copyright violates the Honor Code.

14. Honor Code Reports

Users may report Honor Code concerns through support, dispute tools, document reports, tutor reports, or official email. Reports should describe the conduct, provide available evidence, and identify the related question, document, message, or transaction. Good‑faith reports support platform safety. False or retaliatory reports may lead to enforcement against the reporting account.

Remember: StudyGlade is a learning community. Every user helps maintain integrity by using the platform honestly and encouraging others to do the same.
